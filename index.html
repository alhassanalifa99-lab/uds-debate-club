Who We Are

Nurturing Ghana's Next Generation of Civic Leaders

The UDS Debate and Civic Education Society — a proud member of the UDS Sports Directorate — is a student-led organisation at the University for Development Studies, Tamale. We are committed to developing articulate, informed, and engaged citizens through structured debate, civic education, and community outreach.

Our members come from diverse faculties across UDS, united by a shared passion for public discourse and service to society.

🎤 Public Speaking & Debate

We train members in formal debate formats, rhetoric, and public speaking — skills that serve them for life.

🏛️ Civic Education

We educate communities on democracy, governance, and citizens' rights, from campus to the wider Northern Region.

🏆 Competitions

We represent UDS in inter-university debate competitions across Ghana and internationally.

Purpose & Direction

What We Stand For

Our Mission

To Educate, Empower & Engage

We exist to develop critical thinkers and civic champions at UDS. Through structured debate, public speaking training, and community education, we equip students with the skills to engage meaningfully in Ghana's democratic life.

Our Vision

A Ghana Led by Informed Citizens

We envision a Ghana where every citizen is informed, articulate, and actively participates in public life. The UDS Debate Society strives to produce graduates who carry these values from campus into their careers and communities.

Our History

Built on a Belief That Words Can Change the World

Founded in 2016 at the University for Development Studies, Tamale, the UDS Debate and Civic Education Society was born from a simple but powerful conviction: that students who can think clearly, speak persuasively, and engage respectfully in public discourse are the foundation of a thriving democracy.

Over nearly a decade, we have grown from a small group of enthusiasts into one of the most active student societies at UDS, producing alumni who go on to careers in law, public service, journalism, and civil society.

📝 More to come: The full history of our society will be added here soon. We're collecting stories, photos, and testimonies from founding members and alumni.

What has never changed is our motto: Debate for Development — the belief that informed debate is not just an academic exercise, but a tool for national progress.

Our Core Values

What Guides Everything We Do

Six principles that define every debate, every outreach, every member.

🎯

Integrity

We debate with honesty and argue in good faith. Our positions are grounded in evidence, not empty rhetoric.

🏛️

Civic Responsibility

We believe every citizen has a duty to understand and participate in the governance of their community.

💡

Critical Thinking

We question assumptions, evaluate evidence, and seek truth through structured intellectual engagement.

🤝

Respect

We engage opponents with courtesy and dignity. The strength of an argument wins debates, not the volume of a voice.

🌍

Service

We take our knowledge beyond campus. Community outreach is as central to our identity as competitive debate.

📈

Excellence

We hold ourselves to high standards — in preparation, in delivery, and in the quality of our civic contributions.
